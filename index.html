Su, Junhao is currently a Ph.D. candidate at the Bioinformatics Algorithms Research Laboratory (BAL) in the Department of Computer Science at HKU, supervised by Dr. Ruibang Luo. He received his Bachelor of Engineering and Master of Engineering degrees from HIT. He works on developing algorithms and tools for analyzing genomics data, especially third-generation ONT long-read data, and applications at different medical practices. His research papers have been published in peer-reviewed journals, including Nature Computational Science, Briefings in Bioinformatics, and NAR Genomics and Bioinformatics.

Softwares

► Clair3-Trio: 2.9k downloads, deep-learning, ONT long read, small germline variant calling, bioconda/docker supported
  Built the state-of-the-art variant caller for ONT trio data;
  Under active maintenance, supporting guppy5/Q20+ data, working with a ONT team to integrate it into the ONT analysising pipeline;
  Proposed and applied a new method to bring biological knowledge (via Loss function) into deep-learning models;
  Established a theory that Trio-to-Trio models work best for trio data.

► RENET2: 1.3k downloads, deep-learning, text mining, gene-disease relation extraction, bioconda supported
  Built the first full-text relation extraction model;
  Mined all gene-disease relations from all open-access full-text articles (1.8M) with a large GPUs cluster;
  Applied to find proteins associated with COVID-19.

► ClusterV: deep-learning, ONT long read, HIV sequencing data, Illumina data, drug resistance report, docker supported
  Designed and implemented a new algorithm for finding quasispecies from sequencing data;
  Validated the accuracy of the tool in in silico simulated, biological simulated, and real datasets;
  Applied to find HIV quasispecies from 59 patient samples.

► ONT-TB-NF: ONT long read, Nextflow pipeline, metagenomic samples, ONT adaptive sequencing
  Built a pipeline analyzing TB enrichment from ONT adaptive sequencing and amplicon data;
  Evaluated the performance of adaptive sequencing in metagenomic samples.
